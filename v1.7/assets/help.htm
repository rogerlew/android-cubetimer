Description

Generates scrambles for 2x2x2, 3x3x3, 4x4x4, 5x5x5, 6x6x6, and 7x7x7 cubical puzzle (Rubik's Type Cubes), as well as scrambles for the Megaminx, Pyraminx, Square One, and UFO. It provides an inspection countdown and stopwatch. The provides running statistics of the best, average, and standard deviation of the solve times. Each puzzle will keep it's own running log of statistics.

This application uses the JavaScript scramble algorithms in the scramblers.zip hosted by the World Cube Association (WCA). Credit for the scramble algorithms goes to Jaap Scherphuis, Syoji Takamatsu (Pyraminx), Lucas Garron (Pyraminx), Michael Gottlieb (Pyraminx) Tom van der Zanden (2x2x2), and Clément Gallet. (This project is not affiliated with the WCA).I have made reasonable attempts to contact the authors listed above to request and obtain permission to use their work in this project.

For some of the authors no contact information could be found. If you are one of the authors and do not want your work to be used in this non-commercial open-source project please contact me and I will remove it.

It should be noted that while the code I have written is licensed under Apache 2, and is free to use for both commercial and non-commercial endeavors the scramble code remains intellectual property of their respective owners and should not be used commercially without their consent.

I would like to acknowledge and express my personal gratitude to these Puzzle Gurus for their known or perhaps unknown contributions to this project and the puzzle community at large.


System Requirements

This application run on Android 1.5, 1.6, and 2.0 on small, normal, and large screens. The caveat being the only physical device I have tested it on is my Motorola Droid.

Feedback is welcome and appreciated (rogerlew@gmail.com).


Instructions

  • Tap to start countdown
  • Tap to stop timer
  • App generates new scramble when stopped. (The 2x2x2 scrambler is a little slow.)
  • Long pause on screen to clear records for the current puzzle, or all records can be cleared through the options menu.
  • Use ringer volume controls to adjust beep volume. Beep can been toggled on and off via the options menu.
  • To change the puzzle select "Set Puzzle" under the options menu.
  • The inspection time is applied across all puzzles. Set your preferred inspection time through the options menu.
  • App will remember your records and inspection time automatically.
  • Make sure you exit the timer when you are done, it is configured to keep the screen on.
  • Happy cubing!

Scramblers and Notation

2x2x2

Author(s):
Tom van der Zanden, based on previous work by Jaap Scherphuis
Type:
WCA Compliant uniform random state scrambler
Version:
February 6, 2009
Notation:
Standard 2x2x2 FLUBRD notation is used by this scrambler

3x3x3 - 7x7x7

Author(s):
Jaap Scherphuis
Type:
Random walk scrambler. Not WCA Compliant for 2x2x2 or 3x3x3 cubes (WCA uses random state scrambler), but WCA compliant for 4x4x4 through 7x7x7 cubes.
Version:
January 25, 2009
Notation:
Standard FLUBRD notation is used for the 2x2x2 and 3x3x3 cubes. With 4x4x4 and 5x5x5 cubes this is extended with lower case letters flubrd indicating a turn of an inner slice only, or in multi-slice mode the face letter is followed by a w to indicate a 'wide' move. For even larger cubes, inner slices are denoted by notation.

Tip: On a 2x2x2 cube normally all 6 faces can be turned but if Multi-Slice is on, only the RFU faces are used.

Megaminx

Author(s):
Program by Clément Gallet, based on earlier work by Jaap Scherphuis. Idea by Stefan Pochmann.
Type:
WCA Compliant random walk scrambler
Version:
2008
Notation:
D
all layers below the U face together in one move
R
all layers right from the L face together in one move
++
2/5 move clockwise (144 degrees)
--
2/5 move counterclockwise (-144 degrees)
U
regular move of the U face, according to standard cube notation

Pyraminx

Author(s):
Base script written by Jaap Scherphuis. Javascript written by Syoji Takamatsu. Random-State modification by Lucas Garron in collaboration with Michael Gottlieb
Type:
WCA Compliant uniform random state scrambler
Version:
February 6, 2009
Notation:

Small letter: Rotate a vertex piece

Capital letter: Rotate two layers at once.

UFO

Author(s):
Jaap Scherphuis
Type:
WCA Compliant random walk scrambler
Version:
2006
Notation:

Hold the puzzle so that the yellow part lies directly at the front, light grey on top and dark grey on the bottom. The move B means turn over the right hand side, splitting at the yellow seam. The move A means turn over the right hand side splitting at the pink seam at the front right. The move C means turn over the right hand side splitting at the red seam at the front left. The move U followed by a number between 1 and 5 means turn the top layer clockwise that many steps. Note that the red-blue piece at the bottom left never moves.
